Keynote
Evolving MLOps for IoT: Continuous Learning, Distributed Deployment, and Monitoring


Dr Aftab Khan, Bristol Research and Innovation Laboratory, Toshiba Europe Ltd., UK

Abstract: The integration of machine learning and IoT has created unprecedented opportunities, but it also presents complex challenges in scaling, deployment, and monitoring. This keynote explores the transformative potential of MLOps in IoT systems, emphasizing its evolution and critical components: continuous learning, distributed deployment, and monitoring. We will examine how these practices enable dynamic adaptation to evolving data, support scalable solutions across heterogeneous devices, and ensure reliable performance in diverse environments. Highlighting real-world applications, the talk will address key industry challenges while exploring current gaps and future directions in MLOps for IoT. By identifying opportunities for research and innovation to tackle pressing issues, this session offers a forward-looking perspective on unlocking the full potential of intelligence within future IoT ecosystems, whether you are navigating the technical intricacies of IoT or shaping strategy in this domain.

Bio: Dr. Aftab Khan holds a Ph.D. in machine learning from the University of Surrey, U.K., and is a recognized leader in Distributed AI with over a decade of experience in both academia and industry. Currently the Distributed AI Programme Leader at Toshiba Europe's Bristol Research and Innovation Laboratory, he has contributed significantly to several high-profile EU and EPSRC projects, including EU Horizon REPLICATE, SiDE, TEDDI, and ACASVA, as well as industry-led Innovate UK initiatives like SYNERGIA and CAVShield. Dr. Khan's research focuses on areas such as AI-driven cybersecurity, computational behavior analysis, and pattern recognition. His deep expertise is shaping the future of distributed MLOps frameworks, bridging cutting-edge research with impactful real-world applications.
